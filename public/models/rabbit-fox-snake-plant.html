Protective Plants

ABM · v27

A spatial predator–prey model on a periodic lattice. Plants shelter rabbits, foxes take the exposed, and snakes strike only under cover.

About this model

Foxes eat exposed rabbits; snakes move freely but eat only rabbits sheltering below a plant. The lattice has periodic boundaries. Three presets set the starting state. 1 — Rabbits clear plants: a rabbit removes a plant (probability σ) only when it successfully moves onto a covered site; foxes never clear plants. 2 — Fixed plant cover: plants start at density 0.5 and are frozen — no growth and no removal by any species. 3 — Foxes clear plants: only foxes remove plants, and only when a rabbit is sheltering below. All presets use L=20, γ=1, σ=0.064, μR=μF=μS=10, f=fS=0.1, δF=δS=0.1, and one step per animation frame. Export PNG snapshot saves the current lattice and trajectory as one image.

Parameter search
Search idle. The search always runs L=15, starts each test with 20% plants, 20% rabbits, 20% foxes, and 20% snakes, and caps every trial at 5000 time units.

Small lock buttons beside rate parameters freeze those parameters during search. Unlocked rates are varied around the current winning set. If a new trial keeps all four populations alive longer, it becomes the new winner and is copied to the controls. σ is searched on factor-of-two steps between 0.001 and 1; in this variant σ sets the probability that a rabbit removes a plant when it moves onto a plant-covered site. Rabbit and fox plant-removal toggles are stored with winners and can be locked during search. The rabbit removal trigger (on move attempt vs. on successful move) is held at its current setting during search and stored with each winner.

Freeze plants now locks the current plant cover: no plant growth, no rabbit plant-removal, and no optional fox plant-removal. Press again to unfreeze.

Random-neighbor model sends movement, reproduction, and plant growth targets to a random lattice site instead of a nearest neighbor.
