Model notes

β = 2·ln2 puts the minimum of exp(−βx)−exp(−βx/2) at x = 1. Repulsion exp(−βx) acts as soft excluded volume between all Gabriel-neighbor pairs; the attraction −exp(−βx/2) is added for M–M, Swi6*–Swi6* and Swi6*–M pairs. Backbone bonds are harmonic. Dynamics are overdamped Langevin; the polymer is softly confined to the box and its center of mass is pinned to the center each step. Swi6 leaving the box are forgotten. The Swi6* attraction control scales the entire Swi6*-involving well (repulsion and attraction together) by a factor in units of the M–M depth, which leaves the energy minimum fixed at x = 1 for every attracting pair. For speed, uncharged Swi6 are treated as pure diffusers (excluded from the Voronoi/Gabriel graph and from forces until they charge near an M), and the force-set graph is rebuilt only when a nucleosome or charged Swi6 has moved past a Verlet skin rather than every step. The rate slowdown f divides rec1, rec2 and the spontaneous rate by f. Attraction strengths are toggled separately: M:M nucleosome attraction on (1) or off (0), and Swi6* attraction ×1 or ×2 (applied to both Swi6*–Swi6* and Swi6*–M); the whole well is scaled so the equilibrium distance stays 1 for every attracting pair. Init A telegraph-switches at f=1; Init B locks the state at f=8; Init C is bistable with M:M attraction off (silent domain held by Swi6 bridging alone), using f=2 to deepen the basins. Init D turns M:M on and Swi6* up to ×2: the strong dual attraction keeps it bistable at f=1 even at a larger spontaneous rate.
